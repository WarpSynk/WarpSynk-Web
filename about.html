WarpSynk Protocol FAQ

Find answers to common questions about WarpSynk Protocol, SynkPass NFTs, $WSYNK tokens, and more

General

WarpSynk is a decentralized protocol that gives people control of their digital identity, data, and value. It uses SynkPass NFTs, $WSYNK tokens, SynkNodes, and SynkMesh infrastructure to power identity, data sync, and creator economies.
No single entity owns it. The protocol is community-driven, governed by stewards and a global DAO.
It solves three major issues:
  • Centralized ownership of identity and data
  • Lack of fair economic value distribution in digital ecosystems
  • Fragmentation across platforms, chains, and devices

Identity

It's your on-chain digital ID. It proves ownership, grants access, and links your activity across dApps, games, and social platforms.
No. It is soulbound to your wallet to maintain authenticity and security.
You mint a SynkPass NFT, which generates your SynkID automatically.

Data Sync

It keeps your data, assets, and states synchronized across blockchains, devices, and applications using SolPg on Solana.
A peer-to-peer infrastructure layer of SynkNodes that distribute and verify synchronized data.
Community-run nodes that store, verify, and propagate data. Running a SynkNode requires a SynkID.

Token and Economy

  • Transaction fees within the protocol
  • Staking for governance and rewards
  • Payments in creator and community economies
  • Incentives for running SynkNodes
  • By contributing to the protocol as a steward or node operator
  • By engaging in community projects and creator economies
  • Through airdrops tied to XP systems
A portion of fees is burned to reduce supply over time.

Governance

Reputation, governance power, and protocol rewards.

Security and Privacy

Data is encrypted, verified by SynkNodes, and never controlled by a single party.
